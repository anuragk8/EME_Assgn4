Digital Animation: Photosynthensis Explained

To create an animation for my ePortfolio, I used Pencil2D to sketch key frames and add slight changes to create motion, utilizing vector images of objects like a leaf, the Sun, rays, clouds, and rain. These images were colored to make the animation visually meaningful, and motion was depicted through the movement of the Sun's rays, water droplets, and molecules of Oxygen and Carbon Dioxide. Short captions were added to explain the objects on the screen, and the timing was adjusted for smooth transitions before exporting the video. The voice-over was recorded and synced with the animation timeline in Clipchamp. This project was a valuable educational experience, requiring me to convey a complex scientific concept in a visually engaging way. While Pencil2D is user-friendly but has limitations such as the lack of built-in tweening, making smooth motion transitions time-consuming, Clipchamp simplified the audio editing process though aligning the voice-over with the animation required some trial and error. Overall, the project taught me to balance creativity and technical precision, highlighting the importance of adaptability and problem-solving in multimedia production. Despite the challenges, I appreciated the opportunity to create educational content using accessible tools.
